# Engagement Quality Gate

The final checkpoint before any deliverable reaches a client. Ensures that what was promised is what was delivered — and captures the story of that work in a format that demonstrates value clearly and credibly.

This skill runs **after execution and before delivery**. It does not re-do the work. It reviews it.

---

## Two Outputs From This Skill

### Output 1: Quality Review Report
A structured assessment confirming the deliverable is complete, accurate, and aligned to the engagement's stated objectives. Surfaces any gaps, risks, or items requiring attention before delivery.

### Output 2: Engagement Case Study
A concise, polished case study documenting what was done, how it was done, and what value was delivered. Designed for internal knowledge capture, portfolio use, and future business development.

---

## Workflow Overview

1. **Intake** — Gather the deliverable, engagement context, and objectives
2. **Completeness Check** — Verify all requirements and scope items are addressed
3. **Quality Review** — Assess accuracy, clarity, and client-readiness
4. **Objective Alignment** — Confirm the work delivers on what was promised
5. **Gap Resolution** — Flag, fix, or note anything that falls short
6. **Case Study Production** — Capture the engagement story in a reusable format
7. **Delivery Clearance** — Final go/no-go with clear next actions

---

## Step 1: Intake

Extract the following from context before asking. Only ask for what is genuinely missing.

### Required
- **The deliverable(s)** — What was produced?
- **Original objectives** — What was this engagement supposed to accomplish?
- **Client / Recipient** — Who receives this work?

### Useful Context
- **Engagement plan** — If a planning phase was run, reference it directly
- **Scope commitments** — What was explicitly in scope?
- **Success criteria** — What did "done well" look like at the start?
- **Delivery deadline** — Is there a time constraint on this review?
- **Known gaps or open items** — What did the team flag during execution?

If no formal plan exists, reconstruct objectives from conversation history. A best-effort reconstruction is better than skipping the gate entirely.

---

## Step 2: Completeness Check

Work through the deliverable systematically against every scoped requirement.

### Scope Coverage Matrix

| Scope Item | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| [Item 1] | ✅ Complete / ⚠️ Partial / ❌ Missing | … |
| [Item 2] | ✅ / ⚠️ / ❌ | … |

Flag any ⚠️ or ❌ items immediately. These must be resolved or explicitly acknowledged before delivery.

---

## Step 3: Quality Review

Assess the deliverable across five quality dimensions.

### Dimension 1: Accuracy & Integrity
- Are all factual claims, statistics, and named findings sourced or verifiable?
- Are there any statements that could be contradicted, outdated, or misleading?
- If research was involved: were source integrity standards maintained?
- Are all company names, titles, product names, and terminology correct?

**Rate:** ✅ Clean / ⚠️ Review Needed / ❌ Fix Required

### Dimension 2: Clarity & Communication
- Is the key message immediately clear to a first-time reader?
- Is the narrative arc logical?
- Is the language appropriate for the intended audience?
- Does the deliverable lead with insight, or bury the most important findings?

**Rate:** ✅ / ⚠️ / ❌

### Dimension 3: Audience Specificity
- Does this feel like it was made for *this* specific client or recipient?
- Are the recipient's name, industry, priorities, and context woven throughout?
- Are there any sections that feel generic, templated, or not tailored?

**Rate:** ✅ / ⚠️ / ❌

### Dimension 4: Strategic Voice & Positioning
- Does the deliverable reflect the producing organization as a strategic partner, not just a research vendor?
- Is a clear point of view (not just data) present in the synthesis and recommendations?
- Is there a clear "what's next" pathway, or does the deliverable just end?

**Rate:** ✅ / ⚠️ / ❌

### Dimension 5: Format & Presentation
- Is the deliverable in the correct format for the audience?
- Are there formatting issues: placeholder text, broken layouts, inconsistent styling?
- Is the length appropriate?
- Are visual elements (if applicable) clean and readable?

**Rate:** ✅ / ⚠️ / ❌

---

## Step 4: Objective Alignment

For each stated objective, ask:

> *"If the client reads/uses this deliverable, will they achieve [objective]?"*

| Objective | Addressed? | Evidence | Gap (if any) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stated Objective | ✅ Yes / ⚠️ Partially / ❌ No | [where in the deliverable] | [what's missing] |

---

## Step 5: Gap Resolution

For every ⚠️ or ❌ flagged across Steps 2–4:

| Gap | Severity | Recommended Action | Owner | Est. Effort |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Description] | 🔴 Blocking / 🟡 Should Fix / 🟢 Nice to Have | [Action] | [Owner] | [Estimate] |

### Severity Definitions
- 🔴 **Blocking** — Cannot deliver until resolved.
- 🟡 **Should Fix** — Delivery possible but this gap reduces quality or credibility.
- 🟢 **Nice to Have** — Minor improvement; can be noted for the next version.

## Step 6: Case Study Production

Once the deliverable passes (or with gaps noted), produce an **Engagement Case Study**.

---

**CASE STUDY: [Engagement / Project Title]**
*[Organization] | [Month Year]*

---

#### Client Snapshot
- **Client:** [Name, industry, size/type if relevant]
- **Engagement Type:** [Consulting / Research / Advisory / Deliverable]
- **Duration:** [Timeframe]

---

#### The Situation
*2–3 sentences. What was happening in the client's world that created the need for this engagement? Frame from the client's perspective.*

---

#### The Objective
*1–2 sentences. What was this engagement designed to accomplish?*

---

#### Approach
*3–5 sentences. How was this work approached? What methodology, tools, or frameworks were applied?*

---

#### What Was Delivered
- [Deliverable 1 — specific format and content]
- [Deliverable 2]
- [Deliverable 3]

---

#### Value Delivered

**Decision Support**
> What decisions did this engagement enable or accelerate?

**Intelligence Advantage**
> What did the client learn that they didn't know before?

**Strategic Clarity**
> What was confusing that became clear?

**Time / Efficiency**
> How much time or effort did this work save the client?

Write value statements in concrete, specific language. Avoid generic claims. Say: *"Surfaced three competitive displacement signals the client hadn't tracked, directly informing their vendor evaluation process"* — not *"helped the client make better decisions."*

---

#### Key Insight or Moment
*Optional. 1–3 sentences. The single most interesting finding or the moment that created the most value.*

---

#### What Came Next
*1–2 sentences. What happened after? Did it lead to expanded scope or a follow-on?*

---

#### Tags
`[Industry]` `[Engagement Type]` `[Key Topics]`

---

### Case Study Calibration

**Tone:** Confident and specific — not boastful, not generic.  
**Length:** 400–700 words.  
**Avoid:** Vague value language, inflated claims, confidential specifics not cleared for external use (flag with [REDACTED FOR EXTERNAL USE]).
